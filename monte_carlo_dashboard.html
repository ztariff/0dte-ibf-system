Key Takeaway

The strategy shows strong statistical evidence of a real edge. In 10,000 bootstrap resamples, zero resulted in a total loss and zero had a PF below 1.0. Even at the pessimistic 5th percentile, total P&L was $653K with a PF of 1.79.

The one nuance: the 55.3% win rate alone isn't statistically significant vs 50% (p=0.09). But the strategy's edge comes from asymmetric payoffs — average win ($18.7K) vs average loss ($11.1K) — not just win frequency. The permutation test on mean P&L is highly significant (p=0.00012), confirming the overall edge is real.

Caveat: 161 trades is sufficient to establish statistical significance but not enough to estimate tail risk precisely. The ruin probability analysis shows a 46% chance of seeing a drawdown ≥$100K at some point. More data (out-of-sample testing) would narrow these confidence bands.
